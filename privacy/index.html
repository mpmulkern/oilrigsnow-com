1. Information We Collect

Information You Provide

When you submit a contact form, request a quote, or communicate with us, we may collect:

  • Name and job title
  • Email address and phone number
  • Company name and business address
  • Equipment details you are inquiring about
  • Any other information you choose to share

Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain technical information:

  • IP address and approximate geographic location (country/region)
  • Browser type, version, and operating system
  • Pages visited, time spent, and navigation paths
  • Referring URL and device type
  • Date and time of visits

This information is collected primarily through Google Analytics. See Section 3 for cookie details.

AI Chat Conversations

If you use our AI-powered chat assistant, we collect the content of your conversations. See Section 12 for full details.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Respond to inquiries — answer questions about equipment, pricing, and availability
  • Facilitate transactions — assist with buying, selling, or financing drilling equipment
  • Improve our website — understand how visitors use the site via Google Analytics
  • Communicate with you — send information relevant to your specific inquiries
  • Legal compliance — comply with applicable laws and regulations

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes.

3. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies to operate our website. For full details, see our Cookie Policy. In summary:

  • Google Analytics (_ga, _gid, _gat) — analyze website traffic; data sent to Google under their privacy policy
  • Preference cookies — remember your language choice and cookie consent

You can manage cookies via our consent banner or your browser settings. You can opt out of Google Analytics via Google's opt-out tool.

4. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell your personal data. We may share information in limited circumstances:

  • Google LLC — processes analytics data under a data processing agreement
  • Transaction counterparties — when you initiate a buy or sell inquiry, relevant details may be shared with the other party to facilitate the transaction
  • Legal requirements — when required by law, court order, or to protect rights and safety
  • Business transfers — in the event of a sale or merger of our business; we will notify you before your data becomes subject to a different policy

5. International Data Transfers

Oil Rigs Now is headquartered in Houston, Texas, USA. Our site serves customers worldwide. When we receive data from EEA, UK, or other restricted jurisdictions, we ensure adequate protections are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for transfers to Google LLC and other US-based processors.

By using our website, you acknowledge that your information may be processed in the United States where data protection laws may differ from your jurisdiction.

6. Data Retention

  • Contact inquiries and correspondence — up to 7 years to support transactions and legal obligations
  • Google Analytics data — 26 months (configured in our Analytics account)
  • Cookie consent records — 1 year or until consent is withdrawn
  • AI chat logs — up to 12 months; see Section 12

7. Your Privacy Rights

Regardless of location, you may request to access, correct, or delete personal information we hold about you, and to withdraw consent at any time. Contact us at michael@oilrigsnow.com. We respond within 30 days and may verify your identity before processing requests.

8. European Union — GDPR Rights

If you are in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you have rights under the GDPR or equivalent legislation, including:

  • Access (Art. 15), Rectification (Art. 16), Erasure (Art. 17), Restriction (Art. 18), Data Portability (Art. 20), and Objection (Art. 21)

Legal bases: contract performance, legitimate interests (analytics, security), consent (non-essential cookies), and legal obligation.

You may lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority (e.g., ICO in the UK).

9. California — CCPA/CPRA Rights

California residents have the right to Know, Delete, Correct, and Opt Out of Sale/Sharing. We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. To submit a request, email michael@oilrigsnow.com with subject "California Privacy Request." We respond within 45 days.

10. Canada — PIPEDA Rights

Canadian residents may access their personal information, challenge its accuracy, and withdraw consent at any time, subject to legal restrictions. Contact our Privacy Officer at michael@oilrigsnow.com. Unresolved concerns may be escalated to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

11. Children's Privacy

Our site is for business professionals and is not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. Contact us at michael@oilrigsnow.com if you believe we have done so.

14. California Invasion of Privacy Act (CIPA)

Under the California Invasion of Privacy Act (Penal Code §§ 630–637.9), Oil Rigs Now provides the following specific disclosures:

Google Analytics

Our website uses Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google LLC. Google Analytics receives certain technical data about your visit (including IP address, browser type, pages viewed, and time on site) and processes this data on Google's servers. We use Google Analytics Consent Mode v2, which means Google Analytics does not set cookies or transmit tracking data until you have expressly consented to analytics cookies via the cookie preferences banner on this site. If you decline analytics cookies, Google Analytics operates in a "cookieless" modeling-only mode that does not identify you individually.

AI Chat Interceptions

If you use the AI-powered chat widget on this website, your messages are transmitted to a third-party AI service provider. This constitutes an electronic communication processed by a third party. You are presented with a disclosure before initiating a chat session, and by sending your first message you expressly consent to this transmission, as required by California Penal Code § 631. If you do not consent, you may contact us by phone (713-565-0747) or email (info@oilrigsnow.com) instead.

No Session Replay or Keystroke Capture

Oil Rigs Now does not use session replay tools, keystroke loggers, mouse-tracking software, or any other technology designed to intercept or record your interactions with this website beyond standard analytics (with consent) and AI chat (with disclosure and consent).

Your Rights Under CIPA

California residents who believe their electronic communications have been intercepted without lawful consent may contact our Privacy Officer at michael@oilrigsnow.com or by phone at 713-565-0747.

15. AI Phone Receptionist & Call Recording

Oil Rigs Now uses an AI-powered phone receptionist to answer incoming calls to our main number (713-565-0747). When you call us, your call may be answered by an AI assistant and may be recorded. This section explains what happens and how consent is obtained.

AI Phone Receptionist

Our AI phone receptionist can answer questions about our inventory, services, and how to request a quote. It has the same limitations as our AI chat assistant — it cannot confirm real-time availability, commit to pricing, or bind Oil Rigs Now to any agreement. To speak with a human at any time, say "transfer to a person" or press zero.

Call Recording — Consent & Disclosure

Calls to Oil Rigs Now may be recorded for quality assurance, training, and service improvement. At the start of every call, our AI receptionist provides the following disclosure:

"This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes. You are speaking with an AI assistant. To speak with a human representative, say 'transfer' or press zero."

By continuing the call after this disclosure, you consent to recording. If you do not consent to being recorded, you may disconnect and contact us by email at info@oilrigsnow.com or submit a quote request at oilrigsnow.com/quote.

All-Party Consent States (CIPA & Equivalents)

Several U.S. states require the consent of all parties before a telephone call may be recorded. These include California (CIPA § 632), Florida (§ 934.03), Illinois (720 ILCS 5/14-2), Maryland (§ 10-402), Michigan (MCL § 750.539c), Montana (MCA § 45-8-213), New Hampshire (RSA 570-A:2), Oregon (ORS § 165.540), Pennsylvania (18 Pa. C.S.A. § 5704), Washington (RCW 9.73.030), Connecticut, Delaware, and Nevada. Our mandatory call-start disclosure satisfies the notice-and-consent requirement in all of these states. If you call from one of these states and do not hear the recording disclosure within the first few seconds, please inform our representative immediately.

International Callers

For callers from the EU, UK, and Canada: calls are recorded on the lawful basis of legitimate interests under GDPR (quality assurance, dispute resolution), balanced against your privacy rights. You have the right to object to recording — simply say so at the start of the call. Under PIPEDA, implicit consent is established by the opening disclosure. UK callers are covered by UK GDPR equivalent provisions.

Recorded Call Data Handling

  • Retention: Call recordings are stored for up to 12 months and then deleted.
  • Access: Only authorized Oil Rigs Now personnel may access recordings for quality and dispute purposes.
  • Third-party processing: Our AI phone provider (Retell AI) processes call data under a data processing agreement and may not use recordings for its own purposes.
  • Your rights: You may request access to or deletion of a recording by contacting michael@oilrigsnow.com.
