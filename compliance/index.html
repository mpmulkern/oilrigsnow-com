1. Introduction

Oil Rigs Now, LLC (ORN) — a division of Total Drilling Supply LLC (TDS) — operates as an oilfield equipment broker and consultant serving buyers and sellers across the Americas, the Middle East, Asia, Africa, and Latin America. Our business is inherently international, and that international reach brings with it a responsibility to comply strictly with applicable anti-bribery, anti-corruption, export control, sanctions, and anti-money laundering laws in every jurisdiction where we operate or into which we facilitate equipment transactions.

This notice describes our compliance program commitments. Compliance with these laws is mandatory in every transaction — without exception, regardless of geography, transaction size, or commercial pressure. Our compliance obligations are not negotiable trade-offs against commercial opportunity.

ORN and TDS are committed to conducting all business honestly, ethically, and in full compliance with applicable law.

Any ORN or TDS representative, agent, or counterparty who is asked to participate in a transaction that appears to violate the principles described in this notice is directed to decline and to report the concern as described in Section 8.

2. Anti-Bribery Policy — FCPA

FCPA The United States Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), 15 U.S.C. §§ 78dd-1 et seq., prohibits U.S. persons and companies, and issuers of U.S. securities, from making, offering, promising, or authorizing corrupt payments or anything of value to foreign government officials for the purpose of obtaining or retaining business or securing any improper advantage.

Oil Rigs Now, LLC and Total Drilling Supply LLC do not make, offer, authorize, or approve corrupt payments — directly or indirectly — to any government official, political party, party official, or candidate for political office, at any level of government, in any country, for any purpose.

This prohibition applies to:

  • Direct payments or transfers of value to government officials
  • Payments made through intermediaries, agents, consultants, or joint venture partners, where ORN or TDS knows or has reason to know that the payment will be passed on to a government official
  • Gifts, entertainment, travel, lodging, or charitable donations provided to government officials that are intended to improperly influence a business decision
  • All transactions, regardless of whether they involve U.S. government officials or foreign government officials

Where we engage agents, consultants, or intermediaries in connection with any transaction, those parties are required to certify their compliance with anti-bribery laws and to operate under terms consistent with this policy. We conduct due diligence on intermediaries commensurate with the risk profile of the engagement.

3. UK Bribery Act 2010

UK Bribery Act The UK Bribery Act 2010 applies broadly to companies that carry on business in the United Kingdom or that have connections to UK-based parties. It establishes criminal offences for bribing another person, being bribed, bribing a foreign public official, and — uniquely — the failure of a commercial organization to prevent bribery by persons associated with it.

ORN and TDS have zero tolerance for bribery in any form. This includes facilitation payments — small unofficial payments made to expedite routine government actions — which are prohibited under the UK Bribery Act even where they may be tolerated or customary in certain jurisdictions.

Unlike the FCPA, the UK Bribery Act prohibits not only bribery of public officials but also commercial bribery between private parties. Any payment, gift, hospitality, or other benefit provided to or solicited from any party — public or private — that is intended to induce or reward improper conduct is prohibited.

We maintain adequate procedures within the meaning of the UK Bribery Act to prevent bribery, including this policy statement, due diligence on business partners, and training for relevant personnel.

4. OFAC Sanctions Compliance

OFAC The U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions based on U.S. foreign policy and national security goals. U.S. persons and companies — including ORN and TDS — are prohibited from engaging in transactions with persons and entities on the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons (SDN) List, and from transactions that violate applicable country-based sanctions programs.

ORN and TDS screen all counterparties — buyers, sellers, agents, brokers, and financing parties — against the OFAC SDN List and other applicable U.S. government restricted party lists before entering any transaction. We will not transact with any sanctioned person, entity, or country.

The following U.S. sanctions programs are currently relevant to our business operations. This list is illustrative, not exhaustive, and we update our screening as the sanctions landscape changes:

Iran Comprehensive sanctions — no transactions
Russia Energy sector, VEB, financial sector sanctions
North Korea Comprehensive sanctions — no transactions
Cuba Comprehensive sanctions — no transactions
Syria Comprehensive sanctions — no transactions
Venezuela Energy sector, PDVSA, financial sanctions
Belarus Sectoral and individual sanctions

Sanctions programs change frequently. The list above reflects programs active as of the publication date of this notice. ORN updates its sanctions screening procedures as OFAC designations and programs are modified. Counterparties are responsible for ensuring they are not themselves subject to sanctions and for monitoring applicable restrictions in their own jurisdictions.

In addition to OFAC screening, we may also screen against the EU Consolidated Sanctions List, the UN Consolidated Sanctions List, and the UK Financial Sanctions List where transactions involve parties with connections to those jurisdictions.

5. Export Controls

EAR ITAR Oilfield equipment sold or brokered by ORN and TDS may be subject to U.S. export controls, including the Export Administration Regulations (EAR) administered by the U.S. Department of Commerce Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS), and in certain cases the International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) administered by the U.S. Department of State.

Equipment classification under the EAR is determined by Export Control Classification Number (ECCN) or, if not specifically listed, falls under EAR99. Equipment subject to EAR may require an export license for shipment to certain destinations, end-users, or end-uses — particularly those related to proliferation of weapons of mass destruction, military end-uses in embargoed countries, or certain oil and gas applications in sanctioned regions.

Buyers are responsible for determining whether an export license or other government authorization is required for the export, re-export, or transfer of any equipment they purchase from or through ORN.

ORN reserves the right to require end-use certificates, end-user statements, and supporting documentation for any transaction where export control concerns are present.

Re-export of equipment acquired from ORN to a third country, or transfer of equipment to a third party within the destination country, without appropriate U.S. government authorization may violate U.S. export control law and expose the buyer and any intermediaries to significant civil and criminal liability.

ORN will not knowingly facilitate the export or re-export of controlled equipment to prohibited destinations or prohibited end-users. We reserve the right to request documentation verifying the intended end-use and end-user of any equipment prior to completing a transaction.

6. Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

Oil Rigs Now, LLC and Total Drilling Supply LLC are committed to preventing the use of our business or transactions for money laundering, terrorist financing, or other financial crimes. We conduct risk-based due diligence on counterparties as part of our standard transaction process.

For significant transactions, our due diligence may include:

  • Verification of the buyer's or seller's corporate identity, registration, and principal place of business
  • Identification of ultimate beneficial owners (UBO) of corporate counterparties
  • Proof of funds or source of funds documentation where the transaction size or structure warrants
  • Verification that payment is made from an account held in the counterparty's name at a reputable financial institution
  • Screening of all principals and beneficial owners against OFAC and other restricted party lists

We will not accept cash payments, cryptocurrency transactions, or payments from third parties whose relationship to the transaction is unclear. All payments must be traceable to the disclosed counterparty.

Where we identify suspicious transaction patterns — including unusual payment routing, unexplained third-party involvement, or requests to structure payments in ways that appear designed to obscure their origin or destination — we reserve the right to terminate the transaction and to report the matter to appropriate authorities as required by applicable law.

7. Right to Decline

Oil Rigs Now, LLC and Total Drilling Supply LLC reserve the right to decline any inquiry, listing, transaction, or business relationship at our sole discretion, at any time, without obligation to provide an explanation, where we determine that proceeding would or might:

  • Violate any applicable sanctions program, export control regulation, or anti-bribery law
  • Involve a sanctioned person, entity, or country, even if the direct legal prohibition is not clear
  • Implicate money laundering, terrorist financing, or other financial crime risk
  • Expose ORN, TDS, or our employees to legal, regulatory, or reputational risk
  • Involve an end-use or end-user that raises concern under applicable export control guidance

This right to decline is exercised in good faith and is not a commentary on the legitimacy of any prospective counterparty's business. It reflects our compliance obligations and our conservative approach to transactions that present regulatory uncertainty.

All counterparties are encouraged to consult qualified legal counsel in their own jurisdiction to understand and fulfill their own obligations under applicable law. ORN makes no representation that compliance with this notice satisfies any legal obligation of any counterparty under the laws of any jurisdiction.
