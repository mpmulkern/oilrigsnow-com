Box-on-Box Substructure • DSI Cantilever Mast • NOV TDS-11 Top Drive

OIME Rig #16 is a well-configured 2,000 HP land drilling rig built around a DSI 150-ft cantilever mast rated to 1,000,000 lbs static hook load and a 30-ft box-on-box substructure. Power comes from three Caterpillar 3512C gensets feeding an Omron Hill 4×4 bay SCR house. A NOV TDS-11 500-ton top drive with walking system, three Weatherford MP-16 7,500 psi triplex pumps, a full Swaco solids-control train, and a 10,000 psi BOP stack round out a complete, field-ready package now available in the Houston area.

Capabilities

Key Advantages

01

1,000,000 lb Hook Load Capacity

The DSI 150-ft cantilever mast and matching box-on-box substructure are each rated to 1,000,000 lbs static, providing headroom for deep, high-weight drill string configurations.

02

NOV TDS-11 AC Top Drive with Walking System

The 500-ton NOV TDS-11 operates on AC VFD power at 7,500 psi working pressure and includes a hydraulic walking system — enabling pad drilling without a rig move.

03

Triple-Pump 7,500 psi Mud System

Three Weatherford MP-16 triplex pumps, each driven by dual GE 752 DC motors, deliver high-pressure mud circulation at 7,500 psi — sufficient for demanding formation pressures.

04

CAT 3512C Prime Power — 4,095 KW Total

Three Caterpillar 3512C engines producing 1,365 KW each, managed through an Omron Hill 4×4 bay SCR house, give this rig robust, redundant electrical generating capacity.

05

Complete Swaco Solids-Control Train

Three Derrick 514 linear-motion shakers, a Swaco desander, desilter, and degasser form a full four-stage solids-control system, reducing non-productive time from mud issues.

06

10,000 psi BOP Stack — Dual + Single Ram

A Shaffer-style double-ram and single-ram stack at 10,000 psi, backed by a Hydril 5,000 psi annular and a full CAD closing unit with remote, satisfies well-control requirements for intermediate to deep wells.
