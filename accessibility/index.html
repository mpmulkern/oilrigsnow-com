1. Our Commitment

Oil Rigs Now, LLC — is committed to ensuring that oilrigsnow.com is accessible to all users, including those with disabilities. We believe that access to equipment information, pricing guidance, and brokerage services should not depend on a user's physical or cognitive abilities.

Our target standard is conformance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA, published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). This standard covers a wide range of recommendations for making web content more accessible to people with visual, auditory, cognitive, motor, and speech disabilities.

We treat accessibility as an ongoing commitment, not a one-time checkbox. As we build and improve our platform, accessibility requirements are incorporated into our development and design process from the outset.

2. Conformance Status

Partially Conformant

oilrigsnow.com is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 Level AA. The site was launched in 2026 and active improvements are ongoing. Some content and interactive features may not yet fully meet all AA criteria. We are committed to addressing identified gaps in order of impact to users.

"Partially conformant" means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard. The non-conformances and known limitations are described in Section 4 of this statement. We are actively working to reach full conformance and will update this statement as improvements are made.

3. Accessibility Features Currently Implemented

The following accessibility features are implemented across oilrigsnow.com as of the date of this statement:

Skip-to-content link on every page
Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
ARIA labels on icons, buttons, and form controls
Sufficient color contrast for primary text elements
Responsive text sizing using rem/em units
Proper heading hierarchy (h1 → h2 → h3)
Alt text on equipment images
Visible focus indicators on interactive elements
Keyboard-accessible cookie consent banner
Keyboard-accessible language switcher
Semantic HTML5 landmark elements
Descriptive link text throughout

Skip Navigation

A skip-to-content link appears at the top of every page. It is visually hidden until focused via keyboard, at which point it becomes visible and allows keyboard users to bypass the navigation bar and go directly to the main content.

Keyboard Navigation

All interactive elements on the site — including the navigation menu, equipment search and filter controls, equipment listing cards, contact forms, and cookie consent banner — are reachable and operable using a keyboard alone. Tab order follows a logical reading sequence throughout each page.

Color and Contrast

Primary body text meets the WCAG 2.1 AA minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 against its background. Our gold accent color (#C9A45C) on dark backgrounds meets the 3:1 ratio required for large text and UI components. We continue to audit secondary text elements for contrast compliance.

Heading Structure

Every page begins with a single h1 heading describing the page topic, followed by h2 section headings and h3 sub-section headings where applicable. Heading levels are not skipped. This structure assists screen reader users in navigating page content efficiently.

4. Known Limitations

Despite our efforts, we are aware of the following areas where oilrigsnow.com does not yet fully conform to WCAG 2.1 AA:

  • Third-party content — AI chat widget: The floating AI chat assistant is provided by a third-party vendor. While we have requested that it meet accessibility standards, we cannot guarantee full WCAG 2.1 AA conformance of its interface at this time.
  • Third-party content — Cloudflare scripts: Cloudflare's bot protection and performance scripts may present challenges for certain automated accessibility tools. These are infrastructure-level dependencies outside our direct control.
  • PDF equipment specifications: Some equipment specification sheets linked from listing pages are in PDF format. These PDFs have not been fully audited or remediated for screen reader accessibility. We are working to provide HTML alternatives or accessible PDF versions where feasible.
  • Complex data tables: Some equipment comparison tables may not include all recommended ARIA table markup for screen readers. We are addressing this in ongoing development.
  • Video content: Any video content on the site may not include synchronized captions or audio descriptions at launch. We will add these for all informational video content.

We are actively prioritizing fixes for the limitations above. If you encounter a barrier not listed here, please contact us — user reports help us identify and address issues more quickly.

5. Testing Approach

We use the following methods to evaluate the accessibility of oilrigsnow.com:

  • Self-evaluation against WCAG 2.1 AA success criteria — reviewed during design and development.
  • Manual keyboard navigation testing — completed for all primary user flows including navigation, search, filtering, and form submission.
  • Browser accessibility developer tools — including Chrome Accessibility Inspector and Firefox Accessibility panel.
  • Screen reader spot-testing — using NVDA (Windows) and VoiceOver (macOS/iOS) on key page types.
  • Automated scanning — using axe-core and similar tooling as part of our development workflow.

We have not yet commissioned a formal third-party accessibility audit. A full independent audit by a certified accessibility specialist (CPWA or equivalent) is planned for 2026. Once completed, findings will be incorporated into this statement and remediation will be prioritized accordingly.

7. Accommodation Requests

If you need equipment listing information, specification sheets, or other content from oilrigsnow.com in an alternative format — such as large print, plain text, or an accessible document format — please contact us at info@oilrigsnow.com with the subject line "Accessibility Accommodation."

We will do our best to provide the requested information in a format that meets your needs. For complex technical documents such as equipment specification PDFs, please allow up to 5 business days for us to prepare an alternative format.

We never charge for accessibility accommodations or for providing content in alternative formats.

8. Regulatory Context

We believe web accessibility is a civil right. We work toward compliance with ADA Title III requirements for places of public accommodation as applied to web properties, and we welcome feedback from users and accessibility advocates to help us meet and exceed applicable standards.

For users in the European Union, we acknowledge the European Accessibility Act (EAA, Directive 2019/882) and its requirements for accessible digital products and services. For users in the United Kingdom, we acknowledge the Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations 2018 and related guidance from the Equality Act 2010.

Our accessibility work is not limited to legal compliance — it reflects our belief that everyone who needs oilfield equipment information and brokerage services should be able to access them without barrier.

9. About This Statement

This Accessibility Statement was prepared in May 2026 and reflects the status of oilrigsnow.com as of that date. It is based on self-evaluation using WCAG 2.1 success criteria and manual testing as described in Section 5.

We review and update this statement when:

  • Significant new features or content are launched
  • We receive user feedback identifying new accessibility barriers
  • A formal accessibility audit is completed
  • Applicable legal or regulatory standards change

This statement was last reviewed in May 2026. The next planned review is scheduled for Q4 2026 following our planned third-party audit.
